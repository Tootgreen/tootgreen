Anniversary Gifts That Actually Mark the Occasion

15 June 20257 min readTootGreen

Anniversary gifts are uniquely difficult. You are buying for someone who already has everything you have given them, for an occasion that marks something important, and you want to get it right without it looking like you panicked. We have been through this. Here is what works.

We are based in Kent and we have formed strong opinions about what works as an anniversary gift and what does not, though we will not be sharing which category some of our own gifts have fallen into over the years.

Here is what we have learned.

What an anniversary gift is actually supposed to do

An anniversary gift has a specific job. It is marking time — not just "another year" but "this year, with this person, and everything that means." It is acknowledging that the relationship exists and that someone is glad it does. And it is proving that this was thought about, not just remembered.

Generic anniversary gifts fail because they do the first thing and ignore the rest. Flowers mark the date. A personalised gift marks the relationship. Those are different things, and the difference is visible from the moment it is unwrapped.

For milestone anniversaries — something that lasts

Ten years. Twenty-five. Fifty. These anniversaries deserve something lasting. Something for the home that acknowledges the weight of the occasion.

Our personalised slate boards have become one of our most popular milestone anniversary gifts for exactly this reason. "[Family Name] — Est. [Year]." Something that goes on the kitchen wall and is still there in another decade, still marking the moment, still meaning what it meant when it was given.

"The best anniversary gifts acknowledge the relationship, not just the date. Anyone can remember a date. Fewer people think about what the relationship actually is."

Buying for someone else's anniversary

If you are buying an anniversary gift for friends or family rather than for your own, the challenge is that you are buying for a relationship you are not in. You do not fully know what it is between them. Our suggestion: buy something that references the relationship from the outside. Their names together. The year they got together. Something that says "I remember when this started" — which is, when you think about it, a lovely thing to be able to say.
