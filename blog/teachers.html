What to Get a Teacher (That Is Not Another Candle)

10 July 20256 min readTootGreen

Every July, classrooms across Britain fill up with candles, bath sets and World's Best Teacher mugs that look identical to the one they received last year, and the year before that. We have teenagers. We know how this works. Here is how to actually stand out.

We have been making personalised teacher gifts from our workshop in Kent since 2018, and end of term is one of our busiest periods. We have made thousands of teacher gifts. We have seen what gets kept — sometimes quite literally, because teachers occasionally message us to say the mug is still on their desk three years later — and we have developed some opinions about what works and what ends up in the back of a cupboard.

The thing about teacher gifts is that the bar for standing out is genuinely low. Most gifts are kind but generic. A personalised gift is kind and specific, which is a completely different thing.

Why teacher gifts matter more than people think

Teaching is one of those professions where the feedback loop is genuinely long. A teacher might spend an entire year with a class and never really know what difference they made. The end of term gift — when it is done right — is a moment of explicit acknowledgement. It says: we noticed. You mattered. This year was different because of you.

A generic gift cannot carry that message, no matter how nicely it is wrapped. A personalised one can, because it proves that someone thought about them specifically. That is the thing a World's Best Teacher mug from the shop will never do: it does not prove any thought was involved, because any thought that was involved could have been applied to any teacher anywhere.

The one that works at any budget

Our Thank You Teacher chocolate bar is one of our most consistent sellers, year on year, end of term, every year since we started. A full-size bar with a personalised wrapper — the teacher's name, a message from the class, something specific to the year they had together.

It works because it is fun and it gets eaten, which means it does not add to the pile of things on the teacher's desk that they then have to work out what to do with at the end of the year. It is also, at £3.79, the kind of thing that works brilliantly as an addition to a card, or as the individual contribution when a class is doing a collection for a bigger gift.

The mug they will actually use

Teachers drink a lot of coffee. They drink it cold, reheated, and occasionally at their desk between marking and the next class. A mug that makes them smile when they pick it up is a genuine daily quality-of-life improvement.

The key is specificity. Not "World's Best Teacher" — something that references this teacher, this class, this year. A phrase they always say. A running joke that the class has had all year. Something that makes them laugh when they open it and think of you every single time they use it thereafter. That is the mug that ends up on the desk rather than in the back of the cupboard.

"The best teacher gifts are specific. Not 'you are a great teacher' but 'you made this year matter, and here is the proof.'"

For a very special teacher

Some teachers deserve more than an end of term gift. A teacher who has made a genuine difference over several years — a head of year who went beyond what was required, a form tutor who noticed things, someone who changed how a child saw themselves — deserves something that lasts.

Our personalised slate boards have become one of our most popular gifts for exactly this kind of occasion. Something for their home — their kitchen, their study — that carries their name and a message. Something that is still on the wall in ten years. That is a different category of gift from the end of term token, and it should be, for the teachers who genuinely earned it.
