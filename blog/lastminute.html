Last Minute Personalised Gifts That Do Not Look Last Minute

1 May 20255 min readTootGreen

The occasion is sooner than you thought. This happens. It has happened to us. The birthday you had in the calendar and then somehow missed, the leaving do that crept up, the anniversary that arrived faster than expected. Here is what to do.

We are based in Kent and we make personalised gifts. We dispatch within one to three working days. Royal Mail First Class typically delivers the next working day after dispatch. So if you are reading this on a Wednesday and the thing is on Saturday, there is a reasonable chance we can get something to you in time. Message us to check. We will always tell you honestly.

Here is what to order when you are cutting it close.

Why personalised gifts are actually well suited to the last minute

Here is the counterintuitive truth about personalised gifts and last-minute situations: the fact that it was made specifically for them actually matters more when it arrives slightly late. A generic gift that arrives on time was bought from a shelf. A personalised gift that arrives a day late was made for them — someone typed their name into a box, someone made a thing with that name on it, someone packed it and sent it. The thought is visible regardless of the timing.

We say this not to excuse late gift-buying as a practice, because we have teenagers and we have opinions about organisation, but to point out that the situation is not as bad as it feels at eleven o'clock the night before.

The mug at any speed

Our personalised mugs are one of our quickest standard products to produce. Order early in the day and we will aim to dispatch same day. A mug with their name on it, something that references who they actually are, arriving a day or two after the occasion is still a better gift than something generic that was there on the day. We mean this genuinely.

"A personalised gift that arrives a day late with a genuine explanation is still a better gift than something generic that arrived on time."

If it genuinely cannot arrive in time

Order it anyway. A personalised gift that arrives a day late with a note explaining that you ordered it before the birthday — Royal Mail, the usual — is still a thoughtful gift. A personalised gift delivered as a deliberate "birthday week" extension is actually quite a charming move if you can pull it off with a straight face.
